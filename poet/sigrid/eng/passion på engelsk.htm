poet
ikon

PASSION

I do not want to,
yet I must

Plunge
into cold water

What can I trust?
The beauty of cancer-patients
bereft of superstitious joy,

Their human faces
gave more peace
than others'.

I missed them when they had gone home.
Their treatment had succeeded
or had passed
in vain.
I washed their floors.

Will I be drifting to a cancer-ward
or to poetic sadness
Risk stripping off of all
my superfluous play?

Eyes, eyes

Deep seas to refugees.

Hers wasn't cancer
She had lifelong pain
resulting from childbirth.
